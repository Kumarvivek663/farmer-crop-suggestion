-: Project Description :-

A useful tool that helps farmers choose the best crops based on important soil and environmental factors is the Farmer Crop Suggestion System. This system groups and analyzes data using cluster algorithms, then recommends crops that are specifically suited to the field circumstances.

Key Parameters:

Nitrogen (N): Essential for plant growth, nitrogen levels in the soil influence the type of crops that can thrive.

pH Level: The soil's pH affects nutrient availability. Certain crops grow better in acidic or alkaline soils.

Phosphorus (P): Crucial for root development and energy transfer, phosphorus levels help determine the best crops for a given soil.

Potassium (K): Vital for water regulation and enzyme activation, potassium impacts crop yield and quality.

Humidity: The moisture in the air affects crop health and disease susceptibility.

Rainfall: The amount of rain a region receives dictates the water needs of crops and influences planting decisions.

Temperature: Temperature affects crop growth rates, germination, and overall yield potential.

Methodology:

The technology gathers information from the farmer's field on these characteristics and groups related data points using cluster algorithms like K-means. A set of crop varieties and associated environmental variables are represented by each cluster. Through a comparison of the farmer's unique conditions with these clusters, the algorithm offers customized crop recommendations.

Benefits:

  • Optimized Crop Selection: Farmers receive recommendations tailored to their land's unique characteristics, leading to higher yields and better crop quality.
  • Resource Efficiency: By choosing crops suited to existing conditions, farmers can reduce the need for fertilizers and irrigation, promoting sustainable farming practices.
  • Data-Driven Decisions: The system's use of advanced clustering algorithms ensures that recommendations are based on solid data analysis, increasing the likelihood of successful crop production.
